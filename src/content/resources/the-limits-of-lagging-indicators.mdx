## Accurate, and after the fact

Revenue, margin, and cash position are lagging indicators: they describe results that have already formed. They are precise, auditable, and necessary. They are also, by definition, a view of the past.

A business run entirely on lagging indicators is steering by looking at where it has already been. The information is trustworthy; the timing is the problem.

## What a number leaves out

A margin figure is a single frame of a moving system. It tells you the result, but not the operational conditions (the workflows, hand-offs, and pressures) that produced it. Two identical margins can hide very different operational realities, one stable and one quietly eroding.

Reading the number without reading the system behind it is how a business is surprised by results it could, in principle, have seen forming.

## Pairing lagging with leading

The aim is not to abandon lagging indicators but to pair them with leading ones, the operational signals that move first.

- Lagging indicators confirm what happened
- Leading indicators suggest what is forming
- Read together, they restore the timing that either one alone is missing

Financial systems thinking is mostly this: holding the result and the system that produced it in the same view, so the numbers become something you understand in advance rather than only in retrospect.
